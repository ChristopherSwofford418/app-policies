Child Safety Standards

Jamee by ClearPass Technologies — Last updated: June 2026

Zero-Tolerance Policy: Jamee and ClearPass Technologies have a strict zero-tolerance policy for child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) in any form. This page constitutes our published standards as required by Google Play's Child Safety Standards policy.

1. Prohibition of Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE)

Jamee strictly prohibits any content, behavior, or activity that constitutes or facilitates child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE). This includes, but is not limited to:

  • Child sexual abuse material (CSAM) in any form
  • Grooming, solicitation, or exploitation of minors
  • Any content that sexualizes individuals under the age of 18
  • Sharing, distributing, or requesting sexually explicit content involving minors
  • Any communication intended to exploit or harm children
  • Using the Jamee platform to facilitate contact with minors for exploitative purposes

2. About Jamee

Jamee is an AI-powered documentation and workflow tool designed for healthcare professionals and field service workers. The app helps users automate visit notes, track credentials, and manage schedules. Jamee is a professional productivity tool — it is not a social networking or dating application and is not designed for or marketed to minors.

3. Enforcement

Any user found to be engaging in CSAE activity through or in connection with Jamee will:

  • Have their account immediately terminated
  • Be reported to appropriate law enforcement authorities
  • Be reported to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) CyberTipline where applicable

4. User Reporting

Jamee provides mechanisms for users to report any concerns related to child safety. Users who encounter any content or behavior that may constitute child sexual abuse or exploitation can report it directly through the app or by contacting us at:

Email: info@clearpasstechnologies.com

5. Legal Compliance and Reporting Obligations

ClearPass Technologies complies with all applicable laws regarding child safety, including mandatory reporting requirements under the PROTECT Our Children Act and similar legislation. We will report any known or suspected CSAM to the appropriate authorities, including the NCMEC CyberTipline and relevant law enforcement agencies.

For any child safety concerns, questions about our standards, or to report a potential violation, please contact our designated child safety point of contact:

Our child safety contact is ready and able to speak about Jamee's child sexual abuse material (CSAM) prevention practices and compliance.

7. Updates

These standards will be updated as needed to reflect changes in law, platform requirements, or our practices. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision.
